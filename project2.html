MusicLink

An app to connect music enthusiasts and simplify the music discovery process.

Overview

MusicLink is a music streaming app meant for enhancing the social experience through shared interests in music. Through MusicLink, users can see what their friends are listening, recommend music to their friends, and stay up to date with music recommendations catered to them. Over the course of 6 weeks, I designed MusicLink from with guidance and help from senior designers.

Problem

Music is an essential component of the human experience. People enjoy music and socialization, but user research revealed that people have a hard time expressing their interests in music with friends in a common place. Research showed that the following were problems experienced by users:

  1. Inability to see what their friends are listening to in the moment.
  2. Sharing playlists requires the use of multiple apps.
  3. Unable to see upcoming music events in their area without using multiple apps.

Solution

MusicLink provides an enhanced social experience created through music. Users can create friendships through shared interests in music with other users. Users are also able to discover new music through these interactions with other users in a fun and engaging way.

01 User Research

Survey

At this point of the process we knew we wanted the app to combine socializing and music, but the exact features were unknown. In an effort to create the MVP, we conducted user research. We began with a survey to examine the relationship between general public and their interest in music and socialization We asked about information they might be interested in sharing and seeing from regarding music streaming. We also asked questions about how they currently discover music and how they interested they were in possibly improving this process.

Key insights from survey

  1. Users are generally interested in socializing online through music.
  2. Current services make it difficult to engage in sharing music with others on a single platform.
  3. Not all users want to use Facebook for finding music events and would like a more centralized location.
